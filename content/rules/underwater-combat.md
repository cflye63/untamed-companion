# Underwater Combat

**"The water is not the enemy — but it has no interest in keeping you alive."**

Fighting underwater is about pressure, movement, and oxygen. Not a page of penalties.

---

## 1️⃣ Passive Stamina Drain

Just being submerged is taxing.

**Each turn spent fully submerged costs +1 Stamina** (breath control, pressure, drag).

This applies on top of all other Stamina costs that turn.

> A Breathing Turn still recovers 4 Stamina — but passive drain means **net recovery is +3** while submerged.

**Adapted gear** (aquatic armor, water-breathing equipment) reduces or removes this cost entirely.

---

## 2️⃣ Breath & Survival

### Breath Timer

Hunters can hold their breath for:

```
1 + CON modifier turns
```

The timer counts down each turn you remain fully submerged.

**Surfacing resets the timer instantly.** Surfacing requires upward movement to reach air — this costs normal movement at standard underwater rates.

**A Grappled creature cannot surface.** Grappled means Speed 0. Until the Grapple is broken, the hunter cannot move upward to reach air. Breaking free is the only way to restart the clock.

### Suffocating

At 0 breath turns remaining:

- Lose **5 Stamina per turn**
- If Stamina reaches 0 → lose HP equal to your **CON modifier per turn**

> Water should feel dangerous, not instant death.

### Interaction with Breathing Turns

Taking a Breathing Turn while submerged still recovers 4 Stamina, but the breath timer keeps counting. You are holding still, not surfacing. Use it to buy time — not to solve the problem.

> **Design note:** This means every underwater fight has a hard clock that surface fights do not. Stamina buys rounds; only surfacing stops the countdown. Aquatic encounters should be designed around this pressure — not around HP trading. The longer hunters stay submerged, the more inevitably the fight tips toward the water.

## 3️⃣ Movement in Water

Think of water as slow 3D space.

| Movement | Cost |
|----------|------|
| Any direction (normal move) | +1 Stamina per turn (in addition to base move) |
| Dash | 2× Stamina |
| Upward / Downward | Same cost as horizontal |

No jumping. Vertical movement is swim movement — it costs the same as lateral.

### 3D Positioning

A creature **above you** in the water column has natural leverage:

- Gains **advantage** on shove and grapple attempts against you
- You have **disadvantage** on escape attempts against it

> Aquatic predators almost always drag prey downward before coiling. The position is the kill.

---

## 4️⃣ Combat Adjustments

Keep these light — water changes how weapons behave, not whether they work.

### Weapon Performance

| Type | Adjustment | Reason |
|------|------------|--------|
| Slashing | −1 damage | Water drag resists the swing |
| Piercing | +1 damage | Ideal underwater — water amplifies thrust |
| Bludgeoning | No change | Water transmits force well |

### Ranged Attacks

Bows, thrown weapons, and non-specialized ranged attacks **fail beyond 5 ft** underwater.

**Specialized aquatic gear** (harpoons, bolt throwers) bypasses this restriction.

---

## 5️⃣ Elemental Adjustments

Keep these GM-interpretive rather than hard-coded rules. The goal is feel, not precision.

| Element | Behavior Underwater |
|---------|---------------------|
| **Fire** | Fails entirely |
| **Lightning** | Spreads — GM option: +1 damage die, reduced range |
| **Ice** | Partially forms — useful for barriers and walls, not projectiles |
| **Wind / Air** | Fails or behaves strangely |
| **Water** | May gain +1 die or +1 DC at GM discretion |

> When in doubt: if it makes the scene more dramatic, it works.

---

## 6️⃣ Visibility

Water distorts sound and blurs sight.

**Standard visibility underwater:**
- All Perception checks: **+2 DC**
- Murky or night water: **heavily obscured**

**Exceptions that bypass this:**
- Bioluminescence
- Magical sight
- Sonar / echo-location (certain creatures ignore visual penalties entirely)

---

## 7️⃣ Tactical Guidelines

When hunters try to land or expose an aquatic creature, **always reward the attempt.**

Underwater monsters draw their strength from mobility, depth, and water pressure. Anything that disrupts those — nets, shallow water, collisions, terrain debris, redirected currents — should knock them into a weaker phase or strip one advantage temporarily.

**Clever plans shift tempo. That is the heart of the hunt.**
