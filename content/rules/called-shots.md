# Called Shots & Part Breaks

**Aiming for Weak Points**

---

## What Is a Called Shot?

A Called Shot targets a specific body part instead of the creature's torso.

You declare it before rolling.

- You are **not trying to deal more damage**.
- You are **trying to change how the monster fights**.

---

## Core Rules

When making a Called Shot:

- **Declare the part before rolling**
- Compare your attack roll against that **part's AR**
- On a hit:
  - Deal normal damage
  - Mark progress toward breaking that part

**Breaking a part causes a major behavioral shift.**

## What Happens When a Part Breaks?

When a part reaches its **Break Threshold**:

- The GM narrates the fracture
- The monster loses access to abilities tied to that part
- Movement or tactics may change
- Some parts drop unique materials

**You are reshaping the fight.**

---

## Why Use Called Shots?

Examples:

- **Break wings** to ground a flyer
- **Break legs** to prevent Sprint
- **Break tail** to remove sweep attacks
- **Break crest** to silence roars
- **Break carapace** to lower AR

**You are dismantling survival tools.**

---

## Making Called Shots Easier

Use:

- Steady Aim
- Study Target
- Sharpening Broth (ignores +1 AR)
- Elevation
- Traps
- Forced positioning

**Preparation wins hunts.**
